## Understanding BANT

BANT stands for Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline. This framework has been around for a substantial amount of time and helps sales teams make a determination about whether prospects are worth pursuing.

**Budget** refers to various financial aspects and factors related to whether the prospect has resources available. When you effectively manage this element, you'll see huge increases in close rates.

**Authority** involves identifying the things that matter when determining who makes decisions. Sales managers are crazy about finding the right stakeholders, and this component helps with that process.

**Need** encompasses the different factors that demonstrate whether your solution addresses their pain points. This will increase revenue when implemented properly.

**Timeline** captures numerous elements related to when the prospect plans to make a decision about their purchase.

## Understanding MEDDIC

MEDDIC represents a more comprehensive approach with multiple dimensions: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, and Champion.

**Metrics** involve quantifiable factors and elements that demonstrate value. When sales teams successfully implement this component, they see much better results.

**Economic Buyer** refers to the person who has authority over budget-related things. Your boss wants you to find this stakeholder early.

**Decision Criteria** encompasses the various aspects and factors that influence how prospects conduct an analysis of solutions.

**Decision Process** maps out the numerous steps and elements involved in reaching a purchase decision. This will substantially grow pipeline velocity.

**Identify Pain** captures the different pain points and challenges that create urgency for change. Sales professionals are insane about understanding this component deeply.

**Champion** refers to an internal advocate who provides assistance in navigating the organization and gives consideration to your solution.
