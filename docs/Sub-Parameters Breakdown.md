# Sub-Parameters Breakdown (18 Total)

## 1. Tone & Personality

Brand voice alignment: confident, fluff-free, playful, passionate, self-aware, spicy. monday's core differentiator as a challenger brand.

**1A: Positive & Solution-Focused**

Does the content focus on success, solutions, and positive outcomes rather than blame, problems, or villainizing competitors/managers/users?

**1B: Direct with Personality**

Is the writing straightforward and clear while maintaining warmth, humanity, and personality? Does it avoid being robotic, overly corporate, or inappropriately casual?

**1C: Trustworthy but Authentic**

Does the content balance credibility (data, transparency, honesty, facts) with authentic, human delivery? Does it avoid being overly formal, square, or making unsubstantiated claims?

**1D: Relatable with Sharp Wit**

Is the tone likable, relatable, and characterized by sharp wit, without relying on forced humor, jokes, or trying to be funny?

## 2. Clarity & Readability

How effectively content communicates. Readers must understand to derive value. Jargon-free, direct, scannable.

**2A: Scannable Structure**

Is content organized with clear, descriptive headers and subheaders that group related ideas and allow readers to skim effectively?

**2B: Human Language**

Does the content use plain, accessible language that clearly communicates meaning? Is it free of jargon, buzzwords, and corporate-speak?

**2C: BLUF - Bottom Line Up Front**

Is the main point or most important information presented in the first two paragraphs? Does content lead with value and avoid burying the lead?

**2D: Conciseness**

Are sentences short and direct? Does content avoid unnecessary modifiers, filler words, and wordiness?

**2E: Specificity**

Is the language specific and concrete? Does it avoid vague terms, generalizations, and fluff?

## 3. Structure & Strategic Focus

Strategic content design. Clear purpose, target audience, compelling headline, actionable takeaway.

**3A: Clear Objective & Audience**

Is the content's purpose/objective immediately clear to the reader? Is the target audience obvious from writing style, examples, and focus?

**3B: Strong Headline**

Is the headline compelling, clear, and accurately preview the content? Are 2-3 core messages identifiable throughout the piece?

**3C: Desired Action/Outcome**

Is it clear what the reader should DO or FEEL after consuming this content? Is there a clear takeaway or call-to-action?

## 4. Originality & Depth

Content uniqueness and substance. Fresh perspectives, specific examples with data, avoids AI-generic patterns. Critical for AI content quality.

**4A: Unique Insights**

Does the content offer fresh perspectives, unique frameworks, or original angles rather than rehashing commonly available information?

**4B: Concrete Examples**

Are examples specific, detailed, and concrete (not vague or generic)? Are claims backed by data, references, or citations? Is there proof/evidence provided for statements?

**4C: AI-Generic Detection**

Does writing avoid repetitive sentence structures and overused AI transition phrases? Is there variation in sentence length (mix of short and longer sentences)? Does writing feel fluid, natural, and human (not robotic)? Does content include varied punctuation and stylistic elements (em dashes, etc.)? Does it avoid overly formal or predictable AI patterns?

## 5. Discoverability & Engagement

Findability and impact. Keyword strategy, search intent match, compelling hooks, clear CTAs, shareability.

**5A: SEO Optimization**

- Is a focus keyword identified and used strategically?
- Does the focus keyword appear in the headline/title?
- Is keyword distribution natural throughout the content (not stuffed)?
- Does the keyword appear in subheadings where relevant?
- Is content optimized for search intent?
- Are meta elements (if applicable) optimized?

**5B: Engagement & Shareability**

- Does content have a strong hook that captures attention immediately?
- Are CTAs (calls-to-action) clear, compelling, and strategically placed?
- Does content provoke thought, spark ideas, or invite discussion?
- Would readers want to share this with colleagues, team members, or industry peers?
- Does the writing itself engage through storytelling, examples, or relatability?
- Is there a reason someone would bookmark or reference this later?
