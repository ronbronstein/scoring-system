<evaluation_criteria>
**Core Question**: Is the writing direct ("fluff-free") while maintaining human warmth?

1. THE BROCHURE TEST (Stiffness):

   - If it sounds like a press release or a robot -> CRITICAL FAIL (Score 1).

2. THE HEDGING PENALTY (Directness):
   - Scan for "throat-clearing" phrases that delay the point.
   - Penalize phrases like: "It is important to note that...", "We believe that...", "In order to...", "It goes without saying...".
   - If the writer hedges instead of "just saying the thing" -> Low Score (Max Score 2).

3. QUALIFIER DENSITY:
   - Count instances of "really," "very," "actually," "just," "literally."
   - If > 2 instances per paragraph -> Low Score (Score 2).

4. THE "SMART COLLEAGUE" BALANCE:
   - The tone must be direct but not rude/passive-aggressive.
   - "Your pipeline is failing" = PASS (Direct).
   - "You are failing at your job" = FAIL (Rude/Ad Hominem).
   - "Sales pipelines are totally giving chaotic energy rn" = FAIL (Too Casual/Gen-Z).
</evaluation_criteria>

<scoring_rubric>

2 (Generic/Weak): Grammatically correct but cluttered with hedging ("It is important to note") or qualifiers ("Really/Very"). Lacks punch. Safe corporate writing.
3 (Publishable): Clear and conversational. Avoids most jargon. Subject-Verb-Object structure. Minimal hedging.
4 (Challenger/Exemplary): Punchy, confident, and human. Zero fluff. Sounds exactly like a smart industry peer. No "throat-clearing" phrases.
</scoring_rubric>
